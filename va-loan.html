Common Questions

VA Loan FAQs

VA loans are available to active-duty service members, veterans who served 90+ days in wartime or 181+ days in peacetime, National Guard and Reserve members with 6+ years of service, and surviving spouses of service members who died in the line of duty or from a service-related disability.
Yes! Your VA loan entitlement can be used multiple times throughout your lifetime. If you've paid off a previous VA loan or sold the home and paid off the loan, your entitlement is restored. You can even have two VA loans at once in some circumstances.
The VA funding fee is a one-time fee (typically 1.4%–3.6% of the loan amount) that helps fund the VA loan program. It can be rolled into your loan so you pay $0 upfront. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or higher are exempt from the funding fee entirely.
No. VA loans have more flexible credit requirements than conventional loans. While most VA lenders look for a minimum score of 580-620, many veterans with lower scores still get approved. The VA doesn't set a minimum credit score — it's up to individual lenders. We'll match you with lenders who work with your specific situation.
